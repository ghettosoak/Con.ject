Hi! Welcome to con.ject — the beautiful translator for folks who do lots of micro-translations. This is a quick tutorial to get you up to speed as quickly as possible. Click the points to have a look!

let's go!
1

The left side of the app is for text input. Once you have finished typing, press

ctrl + return cmd + return

to set the translator to task.

However, if you're pasting in text, the translator instinctually know to get to work straight away â€“ no need to press anything.

2

The result will show up on the right side. Press

TAB

to select it, and

ctrl + C cmd + C

to copy it.

Double-click on the background of an unselected result to re-select it.

3

Single-click on a word to get a list of its synonyms.

Double-click on a word to copy it to your clipboard.

4

When selected, you can navigate through results with

↑ & ↓

and return to the corresponding text box with

shift + tab

5

Jump quickly between language directions with

ctrl + ↑ / ↓ cmd + ↑ / ↓

6

Clear blocks of text with the handy cross in the middle.

7

Quickly change languages by clicking on the reference tabs at the edge.

8

Need a more space in one direction than the other? Resize things with the cube in the middle.

9

Go to the main menu by clicking on the circle inside the cube.

10

That's it! Happy translating!
